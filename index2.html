Methodology & Assumptions
Baseline = €0. All values show your net worth vs keeping the current car. Positive = richer.
Early breakeven is correct: You trade a ~€5k car for a €10–24k EV asset. Even after paying the QDVE opportunity cost, the vehicle asset delta is positive from year 1 for most cars. The QDVE deficit catches up over time — Alt3 is the honest long-term view.
EV age factor: Maintenance + repairs grow +8%/yr above CPI. Current car grows +12%/yr (older ICE unreliability).
Depreciation: Front-loaded curve (t^1.6) — matches used EV market data (AutoTrader PT, Autobild 2023-25). Front-loaded = 40–50% of total loss in first 3 years.
Battery degradation: Electricity consumption rises per per-car degrad rate. Leaf (no active thermal mgmt) degrades fastest at 2.8%/yr.
CGT: 28% at year 10 on QDVE portfolio gain only. Applies to both Alt1 and Alt3 consistently. Not applied annually.
Public charging: Effective kWh cost = weighted blend. 12% public @ €0.55 ≈ 3.4× home rate → meaningful add to electricity cost.
⚠ Leaf & Zoe: Thin resale market in Portugal. Residual values may be optimistic. Use the override fields to test haircuts.
Sensitivity band: ±20% on gross return rate (not scaled savings — verified correct).
